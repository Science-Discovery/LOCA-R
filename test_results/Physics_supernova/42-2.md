**(4.1) Dispersion Relation and Phase Velocity**
From Bloch's theorem, the dispersion relation for the periodic structure is given by:
$$ \cos(Kd) = \cos(k_1 a)\cos(k_2 b) - \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{Z_1}{Z_2}+\frac{Z_2}{Z_1}\right)\sin(k_1 a)\sin(k_2 b) $$
where $d=a+b$.
In the long wavelength limit ($K \to 0$), the structure behaves as a homogeneous medium. The phase velocity $v_p=\omega/K$ is given by:
$$ v_p = \sqrt{\frac{\langle\tau\rangle}{\langle\rho\rangle}} = \sqrt{\frac{(a\tau_1+b\tau_2)/(a+b)}{(\rho_1 a + \rho_2 b)/(a+b)}} = \sqrt{\frac{a\tau_1+b\tau_2}{\rho_1 a + \rho_2 b}} $$

$$ v_p = \sqrt{\frac{(a+b)/(a/\tau_1+b/\tau_2)}{(\rho_1 a+\rho_2 b)/(a+b)}} = \frac{a+b}{\sqrt{(\frac{a}{\tau_1}+\frac{b}{\tau_2})(\rho_1 a+\rho_2 b)}} $$
