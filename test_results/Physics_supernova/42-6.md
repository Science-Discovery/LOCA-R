The problem asks to prove that for an ideal black body absorbing radiation perpendicularly, the force per unit area (radiation pressure) $\mathbf{F}_r$ is related to the radiation energy flux density $\mathbf{J}_r$ by the formula 
$\mathbf{F}_r = \mathbf{J}_r/c$.

light in vacuum. The direction of the momentum is the same as the direction of energy propagation.

2.  **Incident Energy:** The radiation energy flux density, $\mathbf{J}_r$, is defined as the energy that flows through a unit area per unit time. Its direction is the direction of energy flow. If we consider a surface of area $A$ on which 
radiation is incident perpendicularly, the total energy arriving at the surface in a small time interval $\Delta t$ is $E = J_r \cdot A \cdot \Delta t$.

3.  **Incident Momentum:** The total momentum carried by this radiation incident on area $A$ during the time interval $\Delta t$ is:
    $P = rac{E}{c} = rac{J_r A \Delta t}{c}$.

4.  **Force from Momentum Transfer:** An ideal black body is defined as an object that absorbs all electromagnetic radiation that falls on it. When the radiation is absorbed, its momentum is completely transferred to the body. According to 
Newton's second law, the force exerted on the body is equal to the rate of change of its momentum. Therefore, the force $F$ on the area $A$ is:
    $F = rac{\Delta P}{\Delta t} = rac{P}{\Delta t} = rac{(J_r A \Delta t) / c}{\Delta t} = rac{J_r A}{c}$.

5.  **Force per Unit Area (Pressure):** The quantity we are looking for, $\mathbf{F}_r$, is the force per unit area. This is also known as radiation pressure. We find it by dividing the total force $F$ by the area $A$:
    $F_r = rac{F}{A} = rac{J_r A / c}{A} = rac{J_r}{c}$.

6.  **Vector Relation:** Since the radiation is incident perpendicularly, the energy flux vector $\mathbf{J}_r$ is normal to the surface. The momentum is transferred in the same direction, so the resulting force is also normal to the surface.

    $\mathbf{F}_r = rac{\mathbf{J}_r}{c}$.
