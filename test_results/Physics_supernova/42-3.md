**（3）求平衡时距离原点$R$处的压强$p(R)$。(Find the pressure $p(R)$ at a distance $R$ from the origin.)**                                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
In hydrostatic equilibrium, the pressure gradient is balanced by the body force per unit volume. The number density of atoms is $n = \rho_0/M$. The force per unit volume is $n\mathbf{F}$.                                                       
$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}R} = n F_R = \frac{\rho_0}{M} \left(-\frac{2\alpha e^2}{(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^5}\right) $$                                                                                                               
Integrating from infinity (where $p=p_0$) to $R$:                                                                                                                                                                                                 
$$ p(R) - p_0 = \int_{\infty}^{R} \frac{\rho_0}{M} \left(-\frac{2\alpha e^2}{(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R'^5}\right) \mathrm{d}R' = \left[ \frac{\rho_0 \alpha e^2}{2M(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R'^4} \right]_{\infty}^{R} $$                
$$ p(R) = p_0 + \frac{\rho_0 \alpha e^2}{2M(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} $$                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**（4）求液氦密度的改变量$\Delta \rho (R)$。(Find the change in liquid helium density $\Delta \rho (R)$.)**                                                                                                                                       
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
The bulk modulus is $B = -V \frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}V}$. Since $\rho = m/V$, $\mathrm{d}\rho = -\frac{m}{V^2}\mathrm{d}V = -\frac{\rho}{V}\mathrm{d}V$. Thus, $\frac{\mathrm{d}V}{V} = -\frac{\mathrm{d}\rho}{\rho}$.                        
This gives $B = \rho \frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}\rho}$. For small changes, $\Delta p \approx \frac{B}{\rho_0} \Delta \rho$.                                                                                                                     
The pressure change is $\Delta p(R) = p(R) - p_0$. So, the density change is:                                                                                                                                                                     
$$ \Delta \rho(R) = \frac{\rho_0}{B} \Delta p(R) = \frac{\rho_0}{B} \frac{\rho_0 \alpha e^2}{2M(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} = \frac{\rho_0^2 \alpha e^2}{2BM(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} $$                                             
Using the approximation for $\alpha$:                                                                                                                                                                                                             
$$ \Delta \rho(R) = \frac{\rho_0^2}{2BM} \frac{M\epsilon_0(\epsilon_r-1)}{\rho_0} \frac{e^2}{(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{32\pi^2 B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 R^4} $$                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**（5）计算被电子吸引过来的液氦的总质量$\Delta M_p$。(Calculate the total mass $\Delta M_p$ of liquid helium attracted by the electron.)**                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
The total attracted mass is the integral of the density change over the volume outside the bubble (from radius $r$ to infinity).                                                                                                                  
$$ \Delta M_p = \int_{r}^{\infty} \Delta \rho(R) \, 4\pi R^2 \, \mathrm{d}R = \int_{r}^{\infty} \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{32\pi^2 B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 R^4} \, 4\pi R^2 \, \mathrm{d}R $$                                               
$$ \Delta M_p = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{8\pi B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2} \int_{r}^{\infty} \frac{1}{R^2} \mathrm{d}R = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{8\pi B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2} \left[-\frac{1}{R}\right]_{r}^{\infty} $$             
$$ \Delta M_p = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{8\pi B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 r} $$                                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**（6）求电子对气泡壁的压强。(Find the pressure exerted by the electron on the bubble wall.)**                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
Due to the uncertainty principle, confining the electron in a bubble of radius $r$ gives it a minimum average momentum magnitude of $p_{el} \approx \frac{h}{2r}$. The kinetic energy of the electron is $K = \frac{p_{el}^2}{2m_e} =             
\frac{h^2}{8m_e r^2}$. The pressure exerted by the electron on the wall can be found from the work done during a change in volume, $P_{el} = -\frac{\mathrm{d}K}{\mathrm{d}V}$. The volume is $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, so $\mathrm{d}V = 4\pi r^2
\mathrm{d}r$.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     
$$ P_{el} = -\frac{\mathrm{d}K/\mathrm{d}r}{\mathrm{d}V/\mathrm{d}r} = -\frac{\frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}r}\left(\frac{h^2}{8m_e r^2}\right)}{4\pi r^2} = -\frac{1}{4\pi r^2} \left(-\frac{2h^2}{8m_e r^3}\right) $$                             
$$ P_{el} = \frac{h^2}{16\pi m_e r^5} $$

**（7）估算平衡时气泡的半径$r$。(Estimate the equilibrium radius $r$ of the bubble.)**                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
The equilibrium radius is determined by the balance of pressures on the bubble's surface.                                                                                                                                                         
-   Outward pressure: electron pressure $P_{el}$.                                                                                                                                                                                                 
-   Inward pressures: ambient pressure $p_0$, surface tension pressure $P_\sigma = \frac{2\sigma}{r}$, and pressure from dielectric attraction (polarization) $P_p = \Delta p(r)$.                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
The equilibrium condition is $P_{el} = p_0 + P_\sigma + P_p$.                                                                                                                                                                                     
Let's estimate the magnitude of each inward pressure term. We'll use the provided constants and assume a radius of $r \approx 2 \times 10^{-9}$ m.                                                                                                
-   $p_0 = 1.0 \times 10^5$ Pa                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
-   $P_\sigma = \frac{2\sigma}{r} = \frac{2 \times 3.7 \times 10^{-4}}{2 \times 10^{-9}} = 3.7 \times 10^5$ Pa                                                                                                                                    
-   $P_p = \Delta p(r) = \frac{\rho_0^2 \alpha e^2}{2BM(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 r^4}$. With $\alpha \approx \frac{M\epsilon_0(\epsilon_r-1)}{\rho_0}$, this is $\frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{32\pi^2 B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 r^4}         
\frac{B}{\rho_0} = \frac{(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{32\pi^2 \epsilon_0\epsilon_r^2 r^4} \approx 3 \times 10^3$ Pa.                                                                                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
Comparing these, $p_0$ and $P_\sigma$ are of the same order of magnitude ($10^5$ Pa), while $P_p$ is about 100 times smaller. Therefore, we can neglect the polarization pressure as it is not a "most important" effect. The correct balance

$$ \frac{h^2}{16\pi m_e r^5} = p_0 + \frac{2\sigma}{r} $$

$$ \frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}R} = n F_R = \frac{\rho_0}{M} \left(-\frac{2\alpha e^2}{(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^5}\right) $$                                                                                                               
Integrating from R to infinity (where the pressure is $p_0$) gives the pressure change $\Delta p(R) = p(R) - p_0$:                                                                                                                                
$$ p(R) = p_0 + \frac{\rho_0 \alpha e^2}{2M(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} $$                                                                                                                                                                   
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**(4) Density Change $\Delta \rho(R)$:**                                                                                                                                                                                                          
Using the definition of the bulk modulus, $B = \rho \frac{\mathrm{d}p}{\mathrm{d}\rho}$, the density change $\Delta \rho$ is related to the pressure change $\Delta p$ by $\Delta \rho \approx \frac{\rho_0}{B}\Delta p$.                         
$$ \Delta \rho(R) = \frac{\rho_0}{B} \left(\frac{\rho_0 \alpha e^2}{2M(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4}\right) = \frac{\rho_0^2 \alpha e^2}{2BM(4\pi\epsilon_0\epsilon_r)^2 R^4} $$                                                               
Using the relation $\alpha \approx \frac{M\epsilon_0(\epsilon_r-1)}{\rho_0}$:                                                                                                                                                                     
$$ \Delta \rho(R) = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{32\pi^2 B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 R^4} $$                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**(5) Total Attracted Mass $\Delta M_p$:**                                                                                                                                                                                                        
This is the integral of the density change over the volume outside the bubble (from radius $r$ to infinity):                                                                                                                                      
$$ \Delta M_p = \int_r^\infty \Delta \rho(R) \, 4\pi R^2 \, \mathrm{d}R = \int_r^\infty \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{8\pi B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 R^2} \mathrm{d}R $$                                                                         
$$ \Delta M_p = \frac{\rho_0(\epsilon_r-1)e^2}{8\pi B \epsilon_0 \epsilon_r^2 r} $$                                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**(6) Electron Pressure on the Bubble Wall:**                                                                                                                                                                                                     
The electron's kinetic energy, due to quantum confinement in a bubble of radius $r$, is $K = \frac{p^2}{2m_e}$ with momentum $p \approx \frac{h}{2r}$. So, $K = \frac{h^2}{8m_e r^2}$. The pressure is $P_{el} =                                  
-\frac{\mathrm{d}K}{\mathrm{d}V}$:                                                                                                                                                                                                                
$$ P_{el} = -\frac{\mathrm{d}K/\mathrm{d}r}{\mathrm{d}V/\mathrm{d}r} = -\frac{-2h^2/(8m_e r^3)}{4\pi r^2} = \frac{h^2}{16\pi m_e r^5} $$

### Part (7) and (8): Numerical Solution                                                                                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**(7) Equilibrium Radius $r$:**                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
The equilibrium is established when the outward pressure from the electron balances the inward pressures. The main inward pressures are the ambient pressure ($p_0$) and the surface tension pressure ($P_\sigma = 2\sigma/r$). The pressure from 
polarization is negligible.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
The balance equation is:                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
$$ P_{el} = p_0 + P_\sigma \quad \implies \quad \frac{h^2}{16\pi m_e r^5} = p_0 + \frac{2\sigma}{r} $$                                                                                                                                            
This equation is solved numerically for $r$.                                                                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
**(8) Effective Mass Ratio:**                                                                                                                                                                                                                     
The total effective mass is the sum of the electron's rest mass ($m_e$), the hydrodynamic mass ($ \Delta M_b = \frac{1}{2}\rho_0 V = \frac{2}{3}\pi \rho_0 r^3$), and the polarization mass ($\Delta M_p$ from part 5).                           
$$ m_{eff} = m_e + \Delta M_b + \Delta M_p $$

import math

rho0 = 125  # kg/m^3

m_e = 9.11e-31  # kg                                                                                                                                                                                                                              
eps0 = 8.854e-12  # F/m                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
h = 6.626e-34  # m^2*kg/s

# Define the function for the pressure balance equation f(r) = 0                                                                                                                                                                                  
def pressure_balance(r):                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
    electron_pressure = h**2 / (16 * math.pi * m_e * r**5)                                                                                                                                                                                        
    inward_pressure = p0 + (2 * sigma / r)                                                                                                                                                                                                        
    return electron_pressure - inward_pressure

r_low = 1.0e-10  # Lower bound for r

    r_mid = (r_low + r_high) / 2                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
    if pressure_balance(r_mid) * pressure_balance(r_low) < 0:                                                                                                                                                                                     
        r_high = r_mid                                                                                                                                                                                                                            
    else:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
        r_low = r_mid                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
r_equil = (r_low + r_high) / 2                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
# --- Part (8): Calculate the effective mass and ratio ---                                                                                                                                                                                        
# Hydrodynamic added mass                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
V_bubble = (4/3) * math.pi * r_equil**3                                                                                                                                                                                                           
Delta_Mb = (rho0 * V_bubble) / 2                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
# Polarization mass (mass of attracted helium)                                                                                                                                                                                                    
Delta_Mp = (rho0 * (eps_r - 1) * e**2) / (8 * math.pi * B * eps0 * eps_r**2 * r_equil)                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
# Total effective mass                                                                                                                                                                                                                            
m_eff = m_e + Delta_Mb + Delta_Mp                                                                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
# Ratio of effective mass to electron mass                                                                                                                                                                                                        
ratio = m_eff / m_e
